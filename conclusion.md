# Conclusion

This project explored how semantic web technologies and Large Language Models can support knowledge graph enrichment for cultural heritage.

Using Wikidata and SPARQL, we investigated the representation of the Mausoleum of Khoja Ahmed Yasawi and analyzed the information currently available in the knowledge graph.

---

# Main Findings

Our SPARQL exploration revealed that Wikidata contains substantial historical and architectural information about the mausoleum.

However, several important aspects remain underrepresented:

- Connection with the Yasawiyya Sufi Order
- Role as a pilgrimage site
- Spiritual and religious significance
- Links with related religious heritage sites

---

# Contribution of LLMs

To address these gaps, we used ChatGPT and Gemini with three prompting techniques:

- Zero-shot prompting
- Few-shot prompting
- Chain-of-Thought prompting

The models successfully generated RDF triples that represented missing information and suggested possible directions for knowledge graph enrichment.

---

# Proposed RDF Enrichment

The final RDF triples introduced new semantic relationships related to:

- Sufism
- Pilgrimage
- Religious heritage
- Cultural networks

These additions would improve the completeness and contextual richness of the Wikidata representation.

---

# Final Reflection

This project demonstrates that Large Language Models can be valuable assistants in knowledge engineering.

However, human evaluation remains necessary to verify accuracy, relevance, and historical validity.

The combination of SPARQL exploration, human expertise, and LLM-assisted RDF generation provides a promising approach for future cultural heritage enrichment projects.

---

# Future Work

Future research could:

- Explore additional cultural heritage entities in Kazakhstan;
- Develop more complex RDF schemas;
- Connect multiple heritage sites within a larger cultural knowledge graph;
- Integrate other semantic web ontologies and linked open data resources.
