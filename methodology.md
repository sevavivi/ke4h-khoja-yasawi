# Methodology

To achieve our goal, we developed a step-by-step methodology for exploring and enriching the Wikidata representation of the Mausoleum of Khoja Ahmed Yasawi.

## 1. Selecting the Topic

We selected the Mausoleum of Khoja Ahmed Yasawi because it is an important example of Islamic and Timurid architectural heritage in Kazakhstan.

The monument is already represented in Wikidata, but we wanted to investigate whether its religious, spiritual, and pilgrimage-related meanings are fully represented as structured data.

## 2. Exploring Wikidata

We started by examining the Wikidata entry of the Mausoleum of Khoja Ahmed Yasawi.

Our aim was to understand what information is already available in the knowledge graph and what information is missing.

We used the Wikidata entity:

**Q46069 — Mausoleum of Khoja Ahmed Yasawi**

## 3. Building SPARQL Queries

We created several SPARQL queries using the Wikidata Query Service.

The queries helped us retrieve:

- existing properties of the mausoleum;
- related entities;
- possible references to Yasawiyya;
- possible references to pilgrimage;
- related heritage sites.

## 4. Analyzing the Results

After running the queries, we compared the results with our research expectations.

We found that Wikidata contains basic information such as location, country, architectural style, material, UNESCO status, and historical period.

However, the results did not clearly show some important cultural and religious aspects.

## 5. Identifying Knowledge Gaps

Based on the SPARQL results, we identified four main knowledge gaps:

1. Missing connection with the Yasawiyya Sufi Order.
2. Missing representation as a pilgrimage site.
3. Missing spiritual and religious significance.
4. Missing links with related religious heritage sites in Kazakhstan.

## 6. Using Large Language Models

After identifying the gaps, we used two Large Language Models:

- ChatGPT
- Gemini

We tested three prompting techniques:

- Zero-shot prompting
- Few-shot prompting
- Chain-of-Thought prompting

The goal was to generate possible RDF triples that could enrich the knowledge graph.

## 7. Human Assessment

We compared the outputs generated by ChatGPT and Gemini.

ChatGPT produced more focused and concise triples, while Gemini provided broader and more detailed explanations.

We manually evaluated the results and selected the triples that were most relevant to our project.

## 8. Creating RDF Triples

Finally, we proposed RDF triples representing missing information about:

- the Yasawiyya Sufi Order;
- pilgrimage function;
- Sufism;
- related religious heritage sites.

These triples were not directly added to Wikidata, but they represent possible knowledge graph enrichment.
