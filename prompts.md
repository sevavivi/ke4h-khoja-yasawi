# Using Large Language Models

After identifying the knowledge gaps, we used Large Language Models (LLMs) to help generate RDF triples that could enrich the Wikidata representation of the Mausoleum of Khoja Ahmed Yasawi.

Our objective was not to automatically insert information into Wikidata, but to evaluate whether LLMs could support knowledge graph enrichment.

---

# Models Used

We compared two Large Language Models:

- ChatGPT
- Gemini

---

# Prompting Techniques

To evaluate the performance of the models, we used three different prompting techniques:

1. Zero-shot Prompting
2. Few-shot Prompting
3. Chain-of-Thought Prompting

---

# Zero-shot Prompting

## Prompt

Generate RDF triples for the Mausoleum of Khoja Ahmed Yasawi that represent:

- Connection with the Yasawiyya Sufi Order
- Role as a pilgrimage site
- Spiritual significance
- Related religious heritage sites

## Results

Both ChatGPT and Gemini successfully identified the four major gaps and generated RDF triples related to:

- Yasawiyya
- Pilgrimage
- Sufism
- Arystan Bab Mausoleum

## Human Assessment

Both models performed well.

ChatGPT produced more concise and focused triples, while Gemini provided longer explanations and additional context.

---

# Few-shot Prompting

## Prompt

Before generating RDF triples, the models were provided with examples such as:

:Mona_Lisa :createdBy :Leonardo_da_Vinci .

:Mona_Lisa :locatedIn :Louvre_Museum .

:Mona_Lisa :depicts :Lisa_Gherardini .

The models were then asked to create similar triples for the Mausoleum of Khoja Ahmed Yasawi.

## Results

The generated triples became more structured and consistent.

Both models followed the example format successfully.

## Human Assessment

Few-shot prompting improved the quality of RDF triples generated by both models.

Gemini showed the greatest improvement.

---

# Chain-of-Thought Prompting

## Prompt

Step 1: Identify missing information.

Step 2: Explain why the information is important.

Step 3: Generate RDF triples.

Step 4: Select the most useful triples.

## Results

The models generated more detailed reasoning before proposing RDF triples.

The outputs contained stronger explanations and better justifications.

## Human Assessment

Chain-of-Thought produced the highest-quality results.

ChatGPT remained closely aligned with the research question, while Gemini generated additional enrichment ideas.

---

# Comparison of Models

| Prompt Type | ChatGPT | Gemini |
|------------|----------|---------|
| Zero-shot | Focused and concise | Detailed and contextual |
| Few-shot | Structured output | Strong adaptation to examples |
| Chain-of-Thought | Most consistent | Most exploratory |

---

# Overall Evaluation

Both models were useful for supporting knowledge graph enrichment.

However, ChatGPT produced the most focused RDF triples for our specific research question, while Gemini provided additional contextual information that could inspire future enrichment work.

The outputs generated by both models served as the basis for the RDF triples proposed in the next stage of the project.
