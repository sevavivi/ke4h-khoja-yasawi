<section>
<h2>Project Topic</h2>
<p>
Our project focuses on the <strong>Mausoleum of Khoja Ahmet Yassawi</strong>, a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Turkistan, Kazakhstan.
</p>
<p>
The monument is important not only as an example of Timurid architecture, but also as a religious, spiritual, and cultural site connected with Sufism and the Yasawiyya tradition.
</p>
</section>

<section>
<h2>Why We Chose This Topic</h2>
<p>
We chose this topic because the mausoleum represents a strong connection between cultural heritage, Islamic architecture, Sufi tradition, pilgrimage practices, and Kazakh and Central Asian identity.
</p>
<p>
From the perspective of <strong>Language, Society and Communication</strong>, this project shows how cultural meaning can be transformed into structured digital knowledge.
</p>
</section>

<section>
<h2>Research Question</h2>
<p>
How can Wikidata for the Mausoleum of Khoja Ahmet Yassawi be enriched through RDF triples and knowledge graph modeling?
</p>
</section>

<section>
<h2>Hypothesis</h2>
<p>
Our hypothesis is that the existing Wikidata entry contains basic historical and architectural information, but does not fully represent the religious, spiritual, and pilgrimage-related significance of the monument.
</p>
</section>

<section>
<h2>Objectives</h2>
<ul>
<li>Analyse the existing Wikidata representation of the mausoleum.</li>
<li>Identify missing cultural, religious, and spiritual information.</li>
<li>Use SPARQL queries to explore structured data.</li>
<li>Compare ChatGPT and Gemini for RDF enrichment.</li>
<li>Propose RDF triples that can enrich the knowledge graph.</li>
</ul>
</section>
